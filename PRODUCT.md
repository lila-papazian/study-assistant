# Product

## Register

product

## Users

University students studying from PDFs, lecture notes, and class materials. They are usually working under time pressure, trying to turn dense course material into usable study assets without losing academic rigor.

## Product Purpose

Study Assistant helps students upload study material, generate summaries, extract key concepts, and prepare for exams more efficiently. The MVP should make the first workflow simple and dependable: add a PDF, process it, and receive a useful summary that supports focused study.

## Brand Personality

Focused, academic, trustworthy. The product should feel like a quiet study workspace: direct, legible, and respectful of the seriousness of coursework.

## Anti-references

Avoid gamification, social features, flashy animations, AI chatbot aesthetics, corporate SaaS dashboards, productivity-guru vibes, and excessive visual clutter. The product should not feel like a chat application or a marketing dashboard.

## Design Principles

- Keep the student in the study flow. Every screen should help them move from source material to understanding with minimal friction.
- Make academic work feel trustworthy. Use clear hierarchy, sober language, and visible process states so students can tell what is happening.
- Prefer workspace clarity over decoration. Visual choices should support reading, comparing, uploading, and reviewing study output.
- Design for exam preparation pressure. Key actions, progress, errors, and next steps should be obvious without requiring exploration.
- Treat AI as a utility, not the interface personality. The product should foreground the student's material and study outcome, not chatbot tropes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Support keyboard-accessible upload and action flows, visible focus states, sufficient contrast, reduced-motion preferences, and layouts that remain usable on laptop and mobile study contexts.

## Product Values

- Student ownership of study materials.
- Local-first when practical.
- Transparent processing.
- No unnecessary cloud dependency.
